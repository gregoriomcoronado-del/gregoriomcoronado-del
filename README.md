## Current Work

### Institutional Decision Infrastructure (IDI)

IDI is a platform architecture designed to model complex organizations as knowledge graphs in order to support decision intelligence.

The architecture identifies institutional entities such as people, projects, grants, datasets, and organizations, then maps the relationships between them to construct a navigable institutional graph.

documents  
↓

↓  
entities  
↓

↓

↓  
interactive exploration  

The first domain implementation of this architecture is:

**RIFI — Research Infrastructure Framework for Institutions**

RIFI applies the IDI architecture to research ecosystems.

## Vision

To make **institutional knowledge visible, connected, and enduring**.

Modern organizations generate enormous data but lack a coherent map of how their people, projects, and resources connect.

Knowledge graphs provide the missing infrastructure needed to support institutional memory and strategic decision intelligence.
